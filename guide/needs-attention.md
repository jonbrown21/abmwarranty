<p>
The <strong>Needs Attention</strong> category highlights devices whose warranty
or AppleCare+ coverage is approaching a meaningful transition and may require
review or action by an administrator. This view is intended to surface
time-sensitive situations before coverage fully lapses.
</p>

<p>
A device is classified as <strong>Needs Attention</strong> when its current
coverage state indicates an upcoming or recent change, including:
</p>

<ul>
    <li>Standard warranty or AppleCare+ coverage that is <strong>expiring soon</strong></li>
    <li>Coverage that has <strong>recently expired</strong> but may still fall
        within a renewal or grace window</li>
    <li>Coverage states that represent a warning or transitional condition,
        rather than clearly in-warranty or fully expired</li>
</ul>

<p>
Devices that are marked as <strong>Inactive</strong> — meaning they are no longer
returned by Apple Business Manager — are intentionally excluded from the
Needs Attention category. Inactive devices are preserved for historical reference
but do not represent actionable coverage decisions.
</p>

<p>
Needs Attention status is evaluated dynamically using the current date and the
most recently imported coverage windows. Devices may move into or out of this
category over time without requiring a new import as dates progress.
</p>

<p>
The Needs Attention count and classification are applied consistently across:
</p>

<ul>
    <li>Dashboard tiles</li>
    <li>Sidebar filters</li>
    <li>CSV exports (when the Needs Attention filter is active)</li>
</ul>

<p>
This logic allows administrators to focus on devices that are nearing coverage
boundaries, supporting proactive renewal decisions, lifecycle planning, and
targeted follow-up without noise from fully expired or inactive inventory.
</p>
