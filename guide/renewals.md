<p>
AppleCare+ coverage may be eligible for renewal for a limited period after
expiration, depending on Apple’s policies and the device’s reported coverage
history. ABM Warranty evaluates renewal-related states using coverage dates
returned directly by Apple Business Manager.
</p>

<p>
Devices whose AppleCare+ coverage has recently expired may be surfaced under
<strong>Needs Attention</strong>. This category is intended to highlight devices
that are approaching or have recently crossed a coverage boundary and may
require review or follow-up.
</p>

<p>
ABM Warranty does not attempt to predict or guarantee renewal eligibility.
Instead, coverage status is derived dynamically from the coverage end date and
the current date, using Apple-provided data only.
</p>

<p>
Once AppleCare+ coverage is determined to be fully expired and no longer within
a transitional window, devices appear under <strong>Expired AppleCare+</strong>
in the Dashboard and filters.
</p>

<p>
Coverage status is recalculated whenever Dashboard views, filters, or device
details are displayed. As a result, renewal-related states may change over time
without requiring a new import.
</p>

<p>
The Dashboard, sidebar filters, and device detail views all reflect this logic
consistently, ensuring that potential renewal-related devices are visible
without misclassifying permanently expired coverage.
</p>

<p>
Administrators should consult Apple Business Manager or Apple documentation
directly to confirm current AppleCare+ renewal eligibility and timelines.
</p>
